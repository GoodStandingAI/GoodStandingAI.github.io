What is the Minute Book Health Monitor?

The Minute Book Health Monitor (MHM) is an automated monitoring and alert system built into Good Standing AI that continuously checks each corporate minute book for completeness and compliance issues‚Äîhelping ensure nothing is overlooked. In a traditional minute book (the binder of corporate records), you need to manually keep key records up to date. MHM helps by scanning your digital minute book and flagging any gaps or irregularities that may need attention.

If something needs attention, the Health Monitor highlights it with clear context and practical next steps. The system provides an at-a-glance compliance status indicator for each entity, allowing you to quickly see if a corporation's records are in good shape or if follow-up is needed.

How the Health Monitor Works

The Health Monitor reviews your company's records against relevant corporate recordkeeping and compliance requirements, based on the information you maintain for the entity. It provides a clear, continuously updated view of what may need review‚Äîwithout requiring manual checklists.

When issues are detected, the system points you to what needs attention and where to address it (for example, a relevant minute book section), then provides guidance to help you resolve the item efficiently.

Comprehensive Compliance Monitoring

MHM monitors all the key records and compliance events that a Canadian corporation is supposed to maintain, including:

  • Governance records: Core corporate documents and approvals
  • Meeting and decision history: Meeting records and written resolutions
  • Registers and key details: Important entity information maintained in the minute book
  • Obligations and deadlines: Items that may require follow-up based on the entity's requirements

By automatically auditing these areas, the Health Monitor ensures your records stay complete and up-to-date, so your corporations remain in good standing without last-minute scrambles.

Coverage Across Canadian Jurisdictions

MHM is designed for Canadian corporate records. Whether your company is federally or provincially incorporated, the Health Monitor adapts based on the entity's jurisdiction and profile.

Coverage and requirements can vary by jurisdiction and by entity type. If you have a question about a specific jurisdiction or scenario, contact us and we'll confirm what's available for your use case.

Configuring the Health Monitor for a Minute Book

Setting up the Minute Book Health Monitor is straightforward. In most cases, the feature is integrated into your entity's minute book view and will begin checking once the company's information and documents are in place. Follow these steps to configure and use the Health Monitor for a given entity:

Step 1: Open the Company's Minute Book

  1. Log in to Good Standing AI and navigate to the entity (company) you want to monitor
  2. Go to the digital minute book for that company from your dashboard or entities list
  3. Click on the company name to view its minute book details
  4. You should see the organized sections of the minute book (such as "Articles", "Bylaws", "Minutes", "Registers", etc.) in the interface

Step 2: Locate the Health Monitor Section

  1. Within the company's minute book area, find the Minute Book Health Monitor panel or tab
  2. It might be labeled as "Health Check", "Compliance Monitor", or simply a compliance status indicator
  3. In some versions, this appears as a dashboard widget with a simple status indicator (e.g., green/yellow/red)
  4. Click on Health Monitor or the status indicator to open the detailed compliance overview for that minute book

Step 3: Verify Company Details

Ensure the basic profile information for the company is correct, since the Health Monitor uses this information to tailor monitoring. In particular, verify:

  • Jurisdiction and entity type
  • Incorporation/anniversary date
  • Key dates relevant to annual obligations (e.g., year-end, if applicable)

Good Standing AI typically captures this information during onboarding (for example, Instant Onboarding uses official records), but it's good to double-check. If anything is missing or incorrect, update those details in the company's settings before proceeding.

Step 4: Enable the Health Monitor

  1. In many cases, MHM is enabled by default for each entity
  2. If you see an option to turn it on or "Configure Health Monitor," go ahead and enable it
  3. This might be a toggle or a button that says "Activate Health Monitor"
  4. Once enabled, the system will perform an initial scan of the minute book
  5. If it's already active, it may have been scanning continuously ‚Äì you'll simply move to reviewing the results

Step 5: Upload and Organize Key Records

For the Health Monitor to give accurate results, make sure your minute book's documents are uploaded and categorized properly. During setup, add any missing foundational documents to their respective sections:

  • Articles section: Upload the Articles of Incorporation to the Articles section
  • Bylaws section: Add the corporate Bylaws to the Bylaws section
  • Minutes section: Include past annual meeting minutes or resolutions
  • Registers section: Upload current director, officer, and shareholder registers
  • Other sections: Add share certificates, organizational resolutions, and other required documents

Good Standing AI's interface is structured by these sections to mirror a traditional minute book, so use that structure. If you have just onboarded a company, you might have some sections empty ‚Äì fill them with the existing records you have. This helps the Health Monitor accurately assess completeness.

Step 6: Review the Initial Health Report

  1. After enabling and providing the documents, let the Health Monitor run an initial check (the scan is usually quick)
  2. You will then see a health report or summary indicating the overall status
  3. Status is shown as a simple indicator like Good, Needs Attention, or At Risk
  4. Review the list of any items flagged, with details and suggested next steps

If something needs review, the report will point you to the relevant area so you can address it quickly.

Step 7: Address Any Flagged Gaps

Now that you have the list of flagged items, take action to resolve them. This step goes beyond configuration into ongoing usage, but it's a critical part of setting up a healthy minute book. For each item flagged:

  • Add records: Upload the needed document(s) to the appropriate section
  • Update details: Refresh outdated information where appropriate
  • Record follow-ups: Upload any supporting documentation needed to complete the record
  • Confirm final versions: Ensure the record reflects the finalized document

For each item, follow the in-app guidance and update the relevant part of the minute book.

As you add or fix items, the Health Monitor will update the compliance status in real-time. You should see the status indicator improve (e.g., the indicator may change to show everything is in good shape once gaps are resolved).

Step 8: Configure Alerts and Notifications

  1. By default, the Health Monitor will always show issues within the app, but you can also set up notifications to stay informed
  2. If the platform offers email alerts or dashboard reminders for compliance issues, choose your notification preferences
  3. Enable alerts for status changes (e.g., when an entity moves to Needs Attention or At Risk)
  4. Set up deadline reminders for upcoming obligations, if applicable
  5. Configure notifications for appropriate team members (e.g., paralegals or lawyers responsible for that client)

Check your user profile or settings for notification options related to compliance monitoring. This way, MHM not only displays issues when you check the app, but actively alerts you to problems so "no deadline is missed" and you can take timely action.

‚úÖ Configuration Complete

Once these steps are done, the Minute Book Health Monitor is fully configured and integrated into your workflow for that entity. You have effectively set up continuous compliance monitoring and issue flagging for the minute book.

Using the Health Monitor Going Forward

After the initial setup, the Health Monitor runs in the background to keep your records in check. There is no heavy maintenance required ‚Äì just update your minute book as you normally would, and the monitor will adjust the compliance status automatically.

Best Practices for Ongoing Use

  • Regular updates: Whenever a corporate update occurs, upload the relevant documents to the minute book. MHM will reflect changes and keep the status indicator current.
  • Dashboard overview: Good Standing AI may show a high-level compliance summary on your main dashboard for all your entities. Use this at-a-glance view to prioritize which minute book to fix first. The goal is to have all entities showing a "Good" status.
  • Guided remediation: When items are flagged, click on them to get more detail. The system often provides guidance or quick links‚Äîe.g., jumping you directly to the relevant section of the minute book.
  • Proactive monitoring: The Health Monitor's status indicator for each entity provides a quick way to identify which companies have issues at any given time, allowing you to address problems before they become urgent.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Health Monitor not updating after document upload:

  • Ensure documents are uploaded to the correct sections
  • Check that file formats are supported (PDF, Word, etc.)
  • Verify that documents are properly executed/signed
  • Allow a few minutes for the system to process and re-scan

Unexpected compliance gaps being flagged:

  • Review the specific gap description for clarification
  • Confirm the relevant records are uploaded and categorized correctly
  • Ensure company profile information (jurisdiction and key dates) is accurate

Notifications not being received:

  • Check your notification preferences in user settings
  • Verify email addresses are correct and not being blocked by spam filters
  • Ensure notification thresholds are set appropriately
  • Contact support if issues persist

What is the Minute Book Health Monitor?

The Minute Book Health Monitor (MHM) is an automated "audit" tool and alert system built into GoodStandingAI that continuously checks each corporate minute book for completeness and compliance issues, giving you peace of mind that nothing is overlooked. In a traditional minute book (the binder of corporate records), you need to manually ensure all required documents and filings are present. MHM does this automatically – it scans your digital minute book and flags any gaps or errors in your records.

If something important is missing, unsigned, or overdue, the Health Monitor will highlight it for you with specific remediation guidance. The system provides an at-a-glance compliance status indicator or score for each entity (like a completion meter showing how complete your minute book is), allowing you to immediately see if a corporation's records are in good shape or if there are issues that need attention.

How the Health Monitor Works

The Health Monitor reviews your company's records against a set of compliance criteria tailored to Canadian corporate law. It provides detailed analysis that goes beyond simple document presence checking – the system actually understands what documents should exist based on your corporation's history, jurisdiction, and compliance obligations.

When issues are detected, the system guides you to fix any problems by identifying exactly what's missing or outdated. For example, it might alert you that "Bylaws not uploaded" or "Last annual minutes were 2 years ago", then provide direct links to upload the required documents or complete the necessary actions.

Comprehensive Compliance Monitoring

MHM monitors all the key records and compliance events that a Canadian corporation is supposed to maintain, including:

  • Foundational documents: Certificate and Articles of Incorporation (or provincial equivalents) are present in the minute book
  • Bylaws and amendments: Ensuring the company's bylaws and any amendments are uploaded and current
  • Registers and ledgers: Confirming that up-to-date registers of directors, officers, and shareholders are maintained
  • Annual meetings or resolutions: Checking that for each year, there are signed minutes or resolutions documenting the annual shareholders meeting (or written resolution in lieu of meeting)
  • Annual filings: Tracking statutory annual returns/filings and warning you if an annual return is due or past due
  • Signed documents: Identifying any important documents that appear to be unsigned or in draft form
  • Other compliance gaps: Detecting anomalies or gaps in the record, such as missing organizational resolutions after incorporation or missing share certificates after share issuance

By automatically auditing these areas, the Health Monitor ensures your records stay complete and up-to-date, so your corporations remain in good standing without last-minute scrambles.

Coverage for All Canadian Jurisdictions

MHM is specifically designed for Canada-wide compliance. Whether your company is federal or provincially incorporated (Ontario, Alberta, British Columbia, etc.), the Health Monitor adapts to that jurisdiction's requirements automatically. GoodStandingAI's features are tailored to Canadian corporate law norms, which means the monitor has built-in knowledge about the common obligations across Canada.

The fundamental records (like articles, bylaws, registers, annual meetings and annual filings) are similar in all Canadian jurisdictions, and the Health Monitor's rules cover these universally. For example:

  • Ontario corporations: The system tracks Ontario annual return deadlines and annual shareholder meeting requirements
  • Alberta corporations: Monitors Alberta's filing and meeting requirements under the ABCA
  • Federal corporations: Follows Corporations Canada requirements and timelines
  • Other provinces: Adapts to each province's specific corporate law requirements

You do not need to configure separate rules for each province – MHM comes with built-in awareness of Canadian compliance tasks across jurisdictions. This ensures you can confidently use the Health Monitor for all your Canadian companies and get relevant gap detection for each one.

🍁 Canadian Focus

GoodStandingAI's initial focus is Canada, so all compliance checks are aligned with Canadian regulations. As the platform expands, it may add support for other countries, but for now you can trust that Canadian corporate compliance is fully covered across all provinces and territories.

Configuring the Health Monitor for a Minute Book

Setting up the Minute Book Health Monitor is straightforward. In most cases, the feature is integrated into your entity's minute book view and will begin checking once the company's information and documents are in place. Follow these steps to configure and use the Health Monitor for a given entity:

Step 1: Open the Company's Minute Book

  1. Log in to GoodStandingAI and navigate to the entity (company) you want to monitor
  2. Go to the digital minute book for that company from your dashboard or entities list
  3. Click on the company name to view its minute book details
  4. You should see the organized sections of the minute book (such as "Articles", "Bylaws", "Minutes", "Registers", etc.) in the interface

Step 2: Locate the Health Monitor Section

  1. Within the company's minute book area, find the Minute Book Health Monitor panel or tab
  2. It might be labeled as "Health Check", "Compliance Monitor", or simply an indicator showing a compliance score/status
  3. In some versions, this appears as a dashboard widget with a status icon (green/yellow/red indicator or percentage showing completeness)
  4. Click on Health Monitor or the status indicator to open the detailed compliance overview for that minute book

Step 3: Verify Company Details

Ensure the basic profile information for the company is correct, since the Health Monitor uses this data to run checks. In particular, verify:

  • Jurisdiction: Federal vs provincial incorporation status (tells the system which rules to apply)
  • Incorporation date: Used to schedule annual compliance events like annual meetings and filings
  • Financial year-end or anniversary date: Important for timing of annual obligations

GoodStandingAI typically captures this information during onboarding (for example, Instant Onboarding pulls public records), but it's good to double-check. If anything is missing or incorrect, update those details in the company's settings before proceeding.

Step 4: Enable the Health Monitor

  1. In many cases, MHM is enabled by default for each entity
  2. If you see an option to turn it on or "Configure Health Monitor," go ahead and enable it
  3. This might be a toggle or a button that says "Activate Health Monitor"
  4. Once enabled, the system will perform an initial scan of the minute book
  5. If it's already active, it may have been scanning continuously – you'll simply move to reviewing the results

Step 5: Upload and Organize Key Records

For the Health Monitor to give accurate results, make sure your minute book's documents are uploaded and categorized properly. During setup, add any missing foundational documents to their respective sections:

  • Articles section: Upload the Articles of Incorporation to the Articles section
  • Bylaws section: Add the corporate Bylaws to the Bylaws section
  • Minutes section: Include past annual meeting minutes or resolutions
  • Registers section: Upload current director, officer, and shareholder registers
  • Other sections: Add share certificates, organizational resolutions, and other required documents

GoodStandingAI's interface is structured by these sections to mirror a traditional minute book, so use that structure. If you have just onboarded a company, you might have some sections empty – fill them with the existing records you have. This helps the Health Monitor accurately assess completeness.

Step 6: Review the Initial Health Report

  1. After enabling and providing the documents, let the Health Monitor scan the minute book (the scan is usually quick)
  2. You will then see a health report or summary indicating the compliance status
  3. This could be a score (e.g., "85% complete") or a simple status like Good, Needs Attention, or At Risk
  4. Review the list of any issues found with detailed descriptions of each flagged item
  5. The monitor will list gaps such as missing documents (with the section name), any unsigned or draft documents, and any overdue tasks

For instance, it might indicate you are missing an annual shareholders' resolution for last year, or that the Directors Register hasn't been updated since a director change.

Step 7: Address Any Flagged Gaps

Now that you have the list of compliance gaps, take action to resolve them. This step goes beyond configuration into ongoing usage, but it's a critical part of setting up a healthy minute book. For each issue flagged:

  • Upload missing documents: Locate and upload the required document to the appropriate section
  • Update records: Refresh outdated registers or information
  • Complete required actions: Draft and upload missing resolutions or minutes
  • Execute documents: Ensure all documents are properly signed and executed

For example, if the Health Monitor indicates "Bylaws not uploaded," locate your company's bylaws document and upload it to the Bylaws section. If it flags an overdue annual meeting, you may need to draft and upload minutes or a resolution for that year (and possibly file the annual return if due).

As you add or fix items, the Health Monitor will update the compliance status in real-time. You should see the status indicator improve (e.g., the compliance score will increase or the indicator color may turn green once all gaps are resolved).

Step 8: Configure Alerts and Notifications

  1. By default, the Health Monitor will always show issues within the app, but you can also set up notifications to stay informed
  2. If the platform offers email alerts or dashboard reminders for compliance issues, choose your notification preferences
  3. Enable email alerts if any entity's health status drops below a certain threshold
  4. Set up deadline reminders for approaching annual filing deadlines
  5. Configure notifications for appropriate team members (e.g., paralegals or lawyers responsible for that client)

Check your user profile or settings for notification options related to compliance monitoring. This way, MHM not only displays issues when you check the app, but actively alerts you to problems so "no deadline is missed" and you can take timely action.

✅ Configuration Complete

Once these steps are done, the Minute Book Health Monitor is fully configured and integrated into your workflow for that entity. You have effectively set up continuous compliance monitoring and gap detection for the minute book.

Using the Health Monitor Going Forward

After the initial setup, the Health Monitor runs in the background to keep your records in check. There is no heavy maintenance required – just update your minute book as you normally would, and the monitor will adjust the compliance status automatically.

Best Practices for Ongoing Use

  • Regular updates: Whenever a new corporate event happens (e.g., you hold an annual meeting, or there's a change of directors), be sure to upload the new documents (minutes, resolutions, forms) to the minute book. MHM will recognize the update and mark that requirement as satisfied.
  • Dashboard overview: GoodStandingAI may show a high-level compliance summary on your main dashboard for all your entities. Use this at-a-glance view to prioritize which minute book to fix first. The goal is to have all entities showing a "Good" status.
  • Guided remediation: When issues are flagged, click on them to get more detail. The system often provides guidance or quick links – for instance, clicking a "missing document" warning might take you directly to the section of the minute book where you can upload that document.
  • Proactive monitoring: The Health Monitor's compliance score for each entity provides a quick way to identify which companies have issues at any given time, allowing you to address problems before they become urgent.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Health Monitor not updating after document upload:

  • Ensure documents are uploaded to the correct sections
  • Check that file formats are supported (PDF, Word, etc.)
  • Verify that documents are properly executed/signed
  • Allow a few minutes for the system to process and re-scan

Unexpected compliance gaps being flagged:

  • Review the specific gap description for clarification
  • Check if annual events are documented for each required year
  • Verify that jurisdiction-specific requirements are being met
  • Ensure company profile information (incorporation date, jurisdiction) is accurate

Notifications not being received:

  • Check your notification preferences in user settings
  • Verify email addresses are correct and not being blocked by spam filters
  • Ensure notification thresholds are set appropriately
  • Contact support if issues persist

Conclusion

The Minute Book Health Monitor is a powerful feature for any Canadian corporate records manager or law firm user. By setting it up for your entities, you establish an early-warning system for compliance that automatically audits your minute books against the requirements across all Canadian jurisdictions, highlighting missing documents, unsigned items, or overdue obligations.

Configuration is simple – mostly about ensuring your data and documents are in place – and once set up, the monitor works continuously in the background. For first-time users, MHM removes the guesswork and manual checklist approach to corporate compliance. Instead of periodically auditing binders or spreadsheets, you get a real-time snapshot of each company's status.

If something needs your attention, the system tells you exactly what it is, so you can fix it promptly. By following the steps in this guide to enable and populate the Health Monitor for each minute book, you'll save time and avoid compliance risks while maintaining the highest standards of corporate record-keeping.

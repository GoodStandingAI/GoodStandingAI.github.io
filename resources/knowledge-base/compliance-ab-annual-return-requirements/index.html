Annual Return Requirements for Alberta Corporations

Essential guide to filing annual returns for Alberta corporations to maintain good standing and comply with provincial regulations.

üìã What is an Annual Return?

An annual return is the yearly filing that Alberta corporations (and some other entities) must submit to the Corporate Registry to keep their company information current and maintain good standing. It updates key details (registered office, directors, and shareholders) as of the corporation's anniversary date.

Legal Requirements & Deadlines

  • ‚Ä¢ Co-operative Associations Act (RSA 2000, c C-28) s. 24(8): Requires Alberta co-ops to file by prescribed date
  • ‚Ä¢ Business Corporations Regulation (Alta Reg 118/2000): Sets filing deadlines and form requirements

üìÖ Filing Deadline

Due by the last day of the month following the corporation's anniversary of incorporation

Example: Corporation incorporated June 15 ‚Üí Annual return due July 31 each year

Failure to file on time will put the corporation in non-compliance and can eventually lead to it being struck off or dissolved by Service Alberta.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who must file an annual return?

Every active Alberta-incorporated company must file one each year. This includes domestic corporations and extra-provincial corporations registered in Alberta (except those home-registered in BC, MB or SK).

What information is required?

The return must list up-to-date company information as of the anniversary date:

  • ‚Ä¢ Names and addresses of directors and officers
  • ‚Ä¢ Registered office address
  • ‚Ä¢ For share-capital corporations: up to five largest shareholders with share percentages
  • ‚Ä¢ Any changes since the last filing must be included

What happens if I miss the deadline?

Consequences of Late Filing:

  • ‚Ä¢ Corporation status shows "overdue"
  • ‚Ä¢ Continued non-filing can lead to strike-off
  • ‚Ä¢ Company name may be struck from register (dissolution)
  • ‚Ä¢ Once dissolved, corporation loses legal standing until revived

Are there fees to file?

Yes. Registry agents charge the prescribed government filing fee (around $75‚Äì80) plus a service charge.

Note: There is no fee for filings by Alberta co-ops under the Co-op Act.
